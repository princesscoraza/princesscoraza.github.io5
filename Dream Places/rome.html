Rome, Italy is a city full of history, culture, and stunning architecture. From the iconic landmarks to hidden gems, Rome offers an unforgettable experience. Here's a look at the top 10 places to visit while in Rome.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is one of Rome's most iconic landmarks, offering a glimpse into the ancient gladiator battles and Roman history.

Vatican Museums

Explore one of the world's largest and most impressive art collections, featuring works by Michelangelo, Raphael, and many more.

Pantheon

A well-preserved ancient Roman temple, now a church, the Pantheon is famous for its massive dome and oculus.

Trevi Fountain

One of the largest and most famous fountains in the world, known for its stunning Baroque design and tradition of throwing coins for good luck.

St. Peter's Square

A grand square located in Vatican City, known for its magnificent colonnades and the impressive St. Peter's Basilica.

Campo de' Fiori

A bustling market square during the day and vibrant nightlife spot at night, perfect for those looking to immerse themselves in Roman culture.

Roman Forum

Walk through the heart of ancient Rome, with ruins of temples, basilicas, and the political center of the Roman Empire.

Gianicolo Hill

Enjoy panoramic views of Rome from this hill, which offers some of the best views of the city's historic landmarks.

Villa Borghese Gardens

A peaceful green oasis in the heart of Rome, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a relaxing afternoon in nature.
